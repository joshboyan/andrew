THINGS THAT HELPED ME

BOOKS

Arm yourself with knowledge—at some point you may need a weapon, and knowledge may be the only one you have.

To me, being able to read books is the single most valuable thing in one’s life. I truly believe that your potential and personality is to be discovered through the lessons that books have to teach you. Sometimes it’s obvious, sometimes it’s not—hell, sometimes the book isn’t even intended to teach you a lesson. Regardless, these are some of the most important books that I have ever read in my life. I want you to discover it for yourself so I won’t tell you why each of these books have helped me so much, but if you feel like you can relate to me in any sort of capacity, regardless of genre I’m confident that each of these books will help and inspire you to achieve whatever it is you want to achieve during your short stay here on earth.

FICTION

  • The Alchemist, Paulo Coelho
  • The Catcher in the Rye, J.D. Salinger
  • The Da Vinci Code, Dan Brown
  • Death of A Salesman, Arthur Miller
  • Farenheit 451, Ray Bradbury
  • Fight Club, Chuck Palahniuk
  • Invisible Monsters, Chuck Palahniuk
  • To Kill a Mockingbird, Harper Lee
  • The Price of Everything, Russ Roberts
  • Twelve Years a Slave, Solomon Northup
  • Uncle Tom's Cabin, Harriet Beecher Stowe

SELF HELP/BUSINESS

  • All You Need to Know About the Music Business, Donald Passman
  • How To Win Friends & Influence People, Dale Carnegie
  • The Inner Game of Tennis: A Guide to the Mental Side of Peak Performance, Timothy Gallwey
  • Purple Cow, Seth Godin
  • Start With Why, Simon Sinek
  • Think and Grow Rich, Napoleon Hill
  • Tribes: We Need You To Lead Us, Seth Godin
  • Turn The Ship Around!, David Marquet
  • The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People, Stephen Covey
  • 21 Irrefutable Laws of Leadership, John Maxwell

SPIRITUAL

  • The Four Loves, C.S. Lewis
  • How We Die: Reflections of Life’s Final Chapter, Sherwin B. Nuland
  • The Question of God, Armand Nicholi

BIOGRAPHIES

CREATIVITY
